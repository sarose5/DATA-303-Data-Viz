Introduction

The primary goal of this project is to analyze people who I know that I run into on a weekly basis at William & Mary. Factors such as when I encountered them, where I encountered them, how often I had seen them during the week of data collection, their gender, and their relationship to me are taken into account. The secondary goal of this project is to present the data in four different ways with three different focuses and see if that reveals any new information. First, I made a scatterplot in Plotly Express that features data points growing and moving to the left depending on when I encountered that person that week, and the size determines how many times I had encountered them that week. The second visualization was made by hand in the same vein as Giorgia Lupi and Stefanie Posavec did for their Dear Data project. This visualization focuses on when I encountered the person I knew. The third and fourth visualizations were made in Tableau, and focuses on where I saw these people over the course of seven days. For the first visualization, I used Tableau's scatterplot and added a bar graph tooltip with summary data on hover-over. For the second graph, I used latitude and longitude coordinates to make a map, and sorted three maps according to the type of person I encountered. I also used color to differientiate between genders and shapes for the different types.


How Many Times Did I Encounter Someone This Week?

This is a Plotly Express scatterplot. On the y-axis I have the fourteen different people I encountered this week, and on the x-axis I have the amount of times I saw them this week. This visualization is meant to show which days I saw them over the course of data collection, and how many times I saw them. Everytime the size of a point increases, that represents another time I saw them that week. This visualization shows the breakdown of people seen each day, and has the most seen people highlighted due to their size. Most people I only saw once or twice during the week, but there were a few people I ran into three times. This visualization was interesting, because the idea of "racing data points" actually came to me from a game played with my friends where our characters had to travel from the left of the screen to the right, and the first one to the right won the game. I thought it would be interesting to try and show the same phenomena with the data points moving to the right and the "winners" being the points with the largest size.


When Did I Encounter People This Week?


This is a visualization I drew myself using pencils and some colored pens. As stated before, I was inspired by the work done on the Dear Data Project. This visualization is meant to show when I saw people over the week of data collection. As written in the "How To Read It" portion, each line represents an hour of my typical day, and the shapes represent an encounter. You can see dry spots in the data around 11am and 1pm, and hotspots around 9am, 3pm and 4pm. I was worried about this visualization, since I am not an artist, but after erasing many drafts and after getting a ruler, it turned out better than I expected.


Where Did I Encounter People This Week?

Tableau Bubbleplot


This first link will take you to a public Tableau dashboard with my third visualization. This visualization aims to focus on where I encountered people during my week of data collection. This is technically another scatterplot according to Tableau, but it is much different than the one from Plotly Express. This visualization reveals that most of the recognized encounters happened in the lobby of my apartment, and around the walkways by the Swem library, Andrews Hall, and Jones Hall. This visualization also includes a tooltip which uses a bar graph to show the gender and type of person breakdown for each area. This visualization was fun to make, and I think the hover tooltips provide good summary data, without being too intrusive.


Tableau Map


This second link will take you to a public Tableau dashboard with my fourth and final visualization. This visualization also focuses on where I encountered people during the data collection week, but in a much more literal sense. This visualization uses latitude and longitude coordinates to show exactly where I had the encounters, as well as the area I was in during the data collection period. The map the visualization uses is also split up between Students, Professors, and Neither, showing a clear difference in where I encounter types of people. The process of making this visualization required getting some coordinate data, but other than that, it was pretty straightforward.
